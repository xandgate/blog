Penn Station smells the same every Sunday. Coffee and floor cleaner and something industrial underneath, the smell of a building that never really closes. I've been making this trip enough times now that I have a routine I didn't consciously design.

Same platform if I can manage it. Window seat, left side, so the sun isn't in my eyes for the New Jersey stretch. Headphones in before I sit down, not because I'm listening to anything in particular but because it signals something — to other people, to myself. I'm not available for conversation. I'm in transit, in more than one sense.

The kids were still awake when I left today. That's been the hardest adjustment — the timing of it. If I leave late enough, I get to do bedtime. If I leave early enough, I don't have that feeling the whole ride up. I haven't found the right answer. I'm not sure there is one.

---

There's a particular quality to the light around Elizabeth. Industrial and flat. Everything is close to everything else. Then it opens up and you're not quite there yet but you can feel the city pulling.

I've been making this commute for months now and I still haven't figured out what to do with the time. I've tried working. I've tried reading. Mostly I look out the window and think about things without finishing the thoughts.

Maybe that's what this space is for.
