This isn't a parenting blog. I have no advice about parenting. I'm mostly improvising, same as everyone else, and the improvisation is going okay with occasional exceptions.

But there are things they say that I catch myself wanting to write down. Not to share. Just to keep. Kids have this way of saying something completely true in a way that adults would never say it, because adults have learned to be careful.

---

One of them asked me last week why I have to go away every Sunday. I explained about the job, about the city, about the fact that it's temporary. They said "but you always come back on Wednesday" as if that settled it. As if the pattern was the point.

I didn't know what to say to that. I'm still thinking about it.

---

The other one is in a phase of asking "is it real?" about everything. Is that story real. Is that animal real. Is the dark real.

I keep trying to give honest answers and then catching myself mid-sentence realizing the honest answer is complicated. Some things are real in some ways and not others. Some things feel real and aren't. Some things are real and feel like they're not.

They don't want that answer. They want yes or no. I've been practicing giving the simple answer.

---

I'll add to this as I remember things. This is just to have somewhere to put them.
