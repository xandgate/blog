I have a professional blog. It's fine. It's useful. I write about things I know well enough to have opinions worth reading.

This is not that.

This is the other thing — the writing I do before I know what I think. The stuff that's too personal for the professional blog and too unfinished for anywhere else. I've been wanting somewhere to put it for a while and I kept not starting because I kept waiting until I had something worth saying.

I'm starting before I have something worth saying. That's the point.

---

The name came from a Monk album. Thelonious Monk used to leave space in his playing — deliberate silence where other pianists would fill in. His band called it "blank on purpose." The space wasn't absence. It was part of the music.

I've been thinking about that kind of intentionality. The things you leave out because you're sure of what you want, not because you don't know. I'm not there yet. But it's what I'm aiming at.

---

I don't know how often I'll write here. I don't have a theme or a cadence or an audience in mind. I'll write when something is sitting with me long enough to need to go somewhere.

If you're reading this: I don't know how you got here, but you're welcome to stay.
